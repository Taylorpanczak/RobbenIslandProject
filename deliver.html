Deliverables/Outcomes

We propose to create a curriculum for late primary school educators to include South African history in their classrooms that will encompass centuries of South African history through an examination of Robben Island’s past. Teaching Robben Island will provide four weeks of live-streamed tours ranging from 1 to 2 hours, to be supplement by content from the Robben Island Museum website and instructional material produced by educators. These lessons will focus on different periods in Robben Island’s history as well as the island’s ecology and geology.

While our curriculum and live streaming will be publicly available, we will partner with school districts in major South African cities like Cape Town, Johannesburg, Pretoria, and Durban to publicize the live streams so that students across South Africa will have access to this important cultural heritage of their own country. We will also partner with school districts in mid-Michigan to pilot and improve the curriculum for future use. Because all of our resources will be public, they can also be used by educators across the Anglophone world. We hope that these resources will be taken up in much of sub-Saharan Africa.
